About this blade

The Evolution OFF-S is Tibhar's offensive workhorse for players who want speed without sacrificing the ability to feel the ball at the table. The 7-ply construction with two Arylate-Carbon layers gives you the rigid, fast core needed for hard topspin loops, while the Koto outer veneer keeps the impact crisp rather than woolly.

This blade rewards a confident, swinging stroke. If you tend to brush the ball or play short over the table, look at the lower-OFF or ALL+ options in the Tibhar range instead.

Who it's for

  • Intermediate to advanced players developing a topspin-driven game
  • Tournament players who already know they prefer a stiffer feel
  • Anyone moving up from a 5-ply all-round blade and ready for more speed

Frequently asked

Is this blade right for an intermediate player?
It can be, but only if you've already played at least 1-2 years and have a developing topspin loop. Intermediate players still working on consistency are usually better served by an ALL or ALL+ blade.
Does the blade come with rubbers?
No, this is a bare blade. You'll need to add rubbers separately. We can glue them on for free if you order both together.
What's the difference between FL, ST and AN handles?
Flared (Konkav) widens at the end, giving a more secure grip. Straight (Gerade) is even thickness top to bottom. Anatomic (AN) has a contoured shape. Most European players use FL.

About this rubber

The FX-P is the softer-sponge sibling of the popular MX-P. The 47.5° medium-hard sponge keeps the dwell time longer than the harder Tibhar rubbers, which translates to more spin variation and easier opening loops from underspin balls. Top sheet stays the same, so you keep the speed when you commit to a stroke.

The semi-tacky surface gives you grip without the deadening feel of fully tacky Chinese rubbers. A solid all-round offensive choice that doesn't punish you on touch shots.

Compared to the MX-P

  • Softer sponge (47.5° vs 50°)
  • Slightly slower in direct shots, but more spin variation
  • Easier to control on serves and short pushes
  • Same top sheet rubber compound

About this glue

Standard latex-based rubber glue. Water-based, VOC-free, ITTF compliant. One bottle is enough for around 12 rubber applications if you use a thin even coat.

How to use it

  1. Apply a thin layer to the rubber sponge with the included sponge applicator
  2. Apply a thin layer to the blade
  3. Wait 10-12 minutes until both surfaces are tacky-dry
  4. Press the rubber onto the blade from one edge, working out air bubbles with a roller
